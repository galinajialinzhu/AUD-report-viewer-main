<SectionHeading title="Executive Summary - 100 Marlin Ave ADU Feasibility (Mill Valley)" icon="clipboard" />

This property is located within the **City of Mill Valley** (not unincorporated Marin County), governed by the Mill Valley Municipal Code (MVMC) Chapter 20.90 for ADU development. The zoning is R-P (Planned Residential), a single-family hillside zone within the SFR-2 General Plan designation (1-7 units/acre). Assessor Parcel Number is 029-231-29.

The existing home is a contemporary 2-story dwelling (~2,700 sqft, built 1981, approximately 28 ft tall) with a 2-car attached garage (~400 sqft). The property sits on a generous 16,335 sqft lot (0.375 acres), but the **defining characteristic of this site is its extreme topography**: an average slope of approximately 43.9%, placing it firmly in steep hillside territory. The underlying geology is Franciscan Complex melange bedrock with Tocaloma-Saurin association soils (low expansivity).

Key development standards under California state law and MVMC 20.90:
- **By-right guarantee**: ADUs up to 800 sqft are guaranteed by-right regardless of site conditions, including steep slopes and AFA constraints. This is the critical regulatory pathway for this property.
- **Max sizes**: 1-bedroom ADU up to 850 sqft; 2-bedroom ADU up to 1,000 sqft; internal conversion (JADU) up to 1,200 sqft.
- **Height**: Detached ADU 16 ft standard; 18 ft with transit bonus (property qualifies at 0.27 mi from transit). Above-garage or attached: 25 ft.
- **Setbacks**: 4 ft side and rear for new construction.
- **Parking**: Not required (transit exemption).
- **Owner occupancy**: Not required for ADU; required for JADU.
- **Mill Valley AFA system**: The Adjusted Floor Area allowance has an estimated 500-900 sqft remaining, but this cannot block the 800 sqft by-right guarantee.
- **Design compatibility**: ADU must match primary dwelling style per MVMC 20.90.050(B).
- **Deed restriction**: Required before final inspection.

The **recommended scheme** is a detached 700-750 sqft ADU on a downhill terrace, utilizing pier and grade-beam foundations anchored into the slope. At under 800 sqft, this qualifies for the by-right pathway with Building Permit Only processing - no ADU Permit hearing required.

<CollapsibleSection title="Mill Valley Hillside ADU - Special Engineering Considerations" defaultOpen={false}>
**Foundation design for 43.9% slope:**
The recommended pier and grade-beam foundation system works by drilling steel-reinforced concrete piers deep into the Franciscan Complex bedrock, then connecting them with reinforced concrete grade beams that span the slope. This creates a stable platform independent of surface soil conditions. Pier depths typically range from 10-30 feet depending on bedrock depth, which the geotechnical investigation will determine.

**Slope stability:**
While Landslide Category 6 indicates low historical occurrence, the 43.9% slope means any construction activity (excavation, drainage changes, vegetation removal) can alter slope stability. The geotechnical report must include a slope stability analysis and may recommend retaining walls, drainage improvements, or limitations on excavation depth.

**Construction logistics:**
Crane access for material placement is likely necessary. Verify that a crane can be positioned on Marlin Ave or the property with adequate reach. Alternative: helicopter delivery for remote hillside sites (rare but used in Marin County for extreme cases). Small equipment (mini-excavators, Bobcats) may be the only option on the slope itself.

**Drainage:**
Critical to manage surface water runoff during and after construction. Disturbed hillside soil is vulnerable to erosion, especially during Marin County's wet winter season (November-March). Construction should plan for erosion control blankets, silt fencing, and temporary drainage during the rainy season.

**Soil characteristics:**
Tocaloma-Saurin association soils have low expansivity, which is favorable (less soil movement with moisture changes). However, these soils over Franciscan Complex melange can be highly variable - the geotechnical investigation may reveal different conditions at different depths and locations across the building pad.
</CollapsibleSection>

<CollapsibleSection title="Regulatory Deep Dive: MVMC 20.90 and State Law Interaction" defaultOpen={false}>
**The 800 sqft by-right guarantee:**
California Government Code Section 65852.2(c) establishes that local agencies must ministerially approve ADUs of at least 800 sqft, 16 ft height, and 4 ft setbacks, regardless of local development standards including FAR, lot coverage, open space, and - critically for this property - hillside overlay requirements. This means Mill Valley cannot deny a conforming ADU under 800 sqft based on AFA (Adjusted Floor Area) calculations, even if the property has exhausted its AFA allocation.

**AFA system interaction:**
Mill Valley's Adjusted Floor Area system limits total development on a parcel based on lot size, slope, and other factors. With an estimated 500-900 sqft remaining AFA, a sub-800 sqft ADU is protected by state law. However, if pursuing Scheme E (>800 sqft), the ADU would be subject to AFA limits and could be denied if AFA is insufficient.

**Design review:**
MVMC 20.90.050(B) requires ADU design compatibility with the primary dwelling. This is an objective standard (materials, colors, roof pitch) and does not constitute discretionary design review. The building department reviews for compliance, not a design review board.

**Deed restriction:**
Per MVMC 20.90, a deed restriction must be recorded before final inspection. This typically restricts separate sale of the ADU, confirms size/use limitations, and may address owner-occupancy requirements for JADUs.
</CollapsibleSection>
