<SectionHeading title="7. Additional Owner Guidance & Considerations" icon="key" />

Utilities and services:
- **Electricity**: 200A panel already installed (2025). Sub-panel for ADU can be fed from existing main panel. Consider separate PG&E meter if tenant will pay own electricity; otherwise, sub-metering or inclusion in rent.
- **Water**: San Jose Water Company service. Likely needs a new water line run to ADU. Separate meter optional but recommended for tenant billing. Water meter installation: $2,000-$4,000.
- **Sewer**: Camera-inspect existing 1964 lateral before construction. If damaged, replace during ADU construction for efficiency. ADU sewer tie-in connects to existing lateral or a new lateral.
- **Gas**: Consider all-electric ADU (induction cooktop, heat pump water heater, mini-split HVAC) to avoid gas line extension and align with California electrification incentives. If gas is desired, extend from existing meter.
- **Internet**: Run conduit for fiber/cable during construction for easy future connection.

Insurance and rentals:
- Notify homeowner's insurance carrier and add landlord endorsement or separate dwelling policy for the ADU. Budget ~$500-$800/yr for additional coverage.
- Consider umbrella liability policy ($1M+) given the rental exposure.
- ADU can be rented long-term (30+ days) without restriction. Verify San Jose's current short-term rental ordinance if Airbnb/VRBO use is desired.
- Tenant screening should prioritize the school-district demographic: families with children are the strongest demand segment and typically the most stable tenants.

Property taxes:
- New ADU construction triggers a supplemental reassessment for the added improvement value only. The existing home's Prop 13 base is not affected.
- Expect ~1.25% of the ADU's assessed improvement value annually (~$3,750/yr for a $300K improvement).
- A supplemental tax bill will be issued after completion to cover the pro-rata remainder of the fiscal year.

<CollapsibleSection title="ADU Design Tips for This Property" defaultOpen={false}>

- **Orientation**: Place the ADU at the rear of the lot, maintaining the 4 ft rear and side setbacks and 6 ft building separation from the main house. Orient the primary living space and windows toward the south or west for natural light.
- **Access**: Provide a dedicated pedestrian path along the side of the house to the ADU entrance. The attached garage and driveway remain with the main house.
- **Privacy**: Position ADU windows to avoid direct sightlines into neighboring yards. Use clerestory windows or frosted glass on the side walls closest to neighbors.
- **Parking**: No parking space required for the ADU. The existing driveway and attached garage serve the main house.
- **Yard preservation**: A 750 sqft single-story footprint will consume most of the rear yard. If outdoor space is important, consider a two-story design (Scheme C) with a ~500 sqft footprint that preserves more yard area, or incorporate a small private patio into the ADU plan.
- **Style**: Mid-century modern or contemporary design will complement the 1964 ranch-style main house while commanding premium rents in this market.
</CollapsibleSection>
