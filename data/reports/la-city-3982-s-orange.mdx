Sensitivity analysis (scenario-level summary, inclusive of soft costs and a 15% contingency):
- Scheme A (Garage Conversion ~550 sf): Total estimated range all-in ≈ $270k – $390k. Conversion construction ~$100k–$150k plus soft costs and contingency. Faster timeline (~6–8 months).
- Scheme B (New Detached ~750 sf): Total estimated range all-in ≈ $300k – $430k. Construction ~$180k–$210k plus soft costs, LID, solar, school fee and contingency. Timeline ~9–12 months.
- Scheme C (Two-story / Garage+ADU): All-in ≈ $370k – $500k. Most complex structure and highest per-sf cost. Timeline ~12–14 months.

Return on investment context:
- A new 2-bed ADU renting for ~$2,500/mo yields ~$30k/yr; at $350k invested, simple payback via rent is ~12 years (not counting appreciation). A smaller 1-bed conversion renting for ~$1,800/mo yields ~$21.6k/yr; recovery in ~13–14 years. Property tax and operating costs (insurance, maintenance) should be incorporated into full financial modeling.

Cost reduction levers:
- Use a LADBS pre‑approved Standard Plan to save design time/cost ($5k–$10k potential).
- Consider pre-fab/modular options cautiously — site work often reduces advertised savings.
- Owner acting as GC or providing sweat equity can reduce contractor overhead but increases management burden and risk.

<TimelineStep step={1} title="Pre-Design & Research" duration="Completed">
  Confirm zoning (R1-1), gather parcel data (APN, lot size), review ZIMAS overlays, and compile MLS imagery and NavigateLA utility maps. Order survey and title report; initiate geotechnical and Alquist‑Priolo fault study as next steps.

</TimelineStep>

<TimelineStep step={3} title="Plan Check Submittal (LADBS ePlanLA)" duration="2–8 weeks">

<TimelineStep step={4} title="Plan Approval & Permit Issuance" duration="2–4 weeks after corrections">
  Address plan check comments and obtain concurrent clearances (Planning, Grading/Geology, Fire, Sanitation). Receive permit once fees paid and corrections cleared. Record any required covenant (rare for ADUs in LA) and schedule pre-construction actions.
</TimelineStep>

<TimelineStep step={5} title="Construction" duration="4–10 months">
  Site prep and demolition (if any), foundation excavation and pour, framing, roofing, rough-in inspections (plumbing, electrical, mechanical), insulation and drywall, finishes (kitchen, bath), exterior hardscape and landscaping, solar PV installation. Expected overall schedule:  
- Garage conversion: ~6–8 months end-to-end (pre-checks + permits + construction).  
- New detached ADU (~750 sf): ~9–12 months typical (geotech/fault study may extend lead time).  
- Two-story / carriage house: ~12–14 months due to added engineering and construction complexity.

<SectionHeading title="6. Additional Owner Guidance & Considerations" icon="key" />

Utilities & services:
- Electricity: Consider 200A upgrade or separate meter. Shared meter is simplest; sub-metering or separate LADWP meter allows tenant billing. LADWP service upgrades and meter installations take scheduling time.
- Water: If sprinklers are required or flow is insufficient, LADWP meter upsizing (1") may be necessary. Use a water flow test early.
- Sewer: Camera-inspect existing lateral; replace or reline if damaged to avoid later backups. Bureau of Sanitation SCAR clearance will be requested at plan check.
- Gas: Consider all-electric ADU (induction cooktop, heat pump water heater) to avoid running new gas lines and to leverage electrification incentives.
- Waste: Request additional sanitation bins if occupancy and waste volume require.

Insurance & rentals:
- Notify homeowners insurance and obtain appropriate landlord endorsements if renting. Consider umbrella liability coverage.
- ADUs are for long-term rentals only in LA (Home-Sharing / short-term rentals prohibited for ADUs). Tenant protections such as Just-Cause apply after 6+ months; ADUs are typically exempt from LA RSO due to being new construction.

Property taxes:
- New construction (the ADU) triggers a supplemental reassessment for the added value only. Expect roughly 1.25% of the ADU’s assessed value annually in property taxes (example: $300k improvement ≈ $3,750/yr). A supplemental tax bill will be issued after completion to cover the remainder of the fiscal year.

Financing & incentives:
- CalHFA ADU Grant Program (subject to availability) can provide up to $40k for eligible homeowners to reimburse pre-development costs. Investigate eligibility and timelines early.
- LADWP and SoCalREN rebates apply for efficient HVAC, heat pump water heaters, and other energy improvements. Solar PV on ADU is required by Title 24 for new detached ADUs; 30% federal tax credit may apply.
- Consider Section 8 or other subsidized programs if owner is open to affordable housing options, which may have additional incentives.

</Callout>

<CollapsibleSection title="Supplementary Commercial Analysis & Tech Appendix (Translated and Summarized)" defaultOpen={false}>
Below is a translation and concise summary of the additional commercial / product strategy content included with the original materials, preserved here for completeness and future strategic planning.

- Problem statement: Municipal zoning and development rules are deeply fragmented—ArcGIS services, scanned PDFs, and county/city portals produce highly non-standard, distributed data that is costly to transform into actionable feasibility information for ADUs.
- Technical approach: Combine automated ArcGIS REST API scraping to capture geometry (parcel polygons, zoning layers) with LLM-driven parsing of unstructured regulatory PDFs (setback rules, overlays, exceptions) to produce a unified JSON schema that binds spatial geometry to rule logic. This enables true, automated micro-feasibility analysis (e.g., “Given parcel polygon + recorded easements + setbacks + AP zone buffer, can a 750 sf ADU sit here?”).
- Business logic: The moat is in processing “dirty” municipal APIs and dense scanned legislative text at scale and keeping the dataset live as rules and portals change. Productization pathways include:
  - To-B (SaaS/API): sell standardized zoning+feasibility APIs to architects, builders, and design platforms.
  - To-C (Lead Gen): consumer-facing ADU feasibility checker that generates developer leads for construction partners.
- Risks: Legal liability for incorrect automated feasibility outputs, the need for human-in-the-loop review for borderline cases, and the engineering cost of maintaining brittle scrapers against evolving municipal systems.
- Differentiation: Focus tightly on residential ADU micro-scenarios in high-value jurisdictions (e.g., core California cities) rather than attempting broad national coverage; combine geometry + nuance-driven NLP to extract conditional rules (IF/THEN exceptions).
- Data schema note: The field design (schema) is critical — it must represent polygons, setbacks, exceptions, recorded easement geometries, per-parcel overlay flags, and the logical precedence of rules (state law overrides local rules, etc.). A robust schema enables programmatic "can this footprint be sited here?" calculations that power design automation and lead generation.
